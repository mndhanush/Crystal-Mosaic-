General & Shop Questions

Information regarding custom orders, glass care, and shop availability.

While we do not take fully custom window commissions via the website, we are happy to accept color-swap requests for our existing suncatcher designs. If you see a botanical or geometric pattern you like but want it in different colors, please contact us!

Dust your glass lightly with a microfiber cloth. Do not use Windex, ammonia, or harsh chemical cleaners, as these will strip the protective wax and oxidize the solder lines. If deeper cleaning is needed, use a mild, non-abrasive dish soap and water. You can apply a light coat of carnauba car wax once a year to keep the solder lines shining.

Every item is cut, foiled, and soldered by hand in our small studio. Because of the labor-intensive nature of stained glass, we release items in small batches. We recommend signing up for our newsletter to be notified exactly when a "Shop Drop" goes live.

Restoration & Repairs

Common questions about estimating and fixing broken windows.

Because every window is unique, we cannot offer flat-rate repair pricing. Costs depend on the severity of the damage, the rarity of the glass that needs matching, and whether the panel must be entirely dismantled (re-leaded) or just spot-repaired. Please use our Estimate Form to send photos for an accurate quote.

Minor "drop-in" repairs (like replacing one or two edge pieces) can sometimes be done on-site. However, major structural repairs, bowing, or central glass breakage require us to safely remove the window and bring it back to our studio workspace.

Classes & Workshops

Logistics, safety, and booking details for our in-studio sessions.

Stained glass is very safe when proper protocols are followed. You do not need to bring any gear. We provide safety glasses, cut-resistant gloves, and individual fume extractors for soldering. However, we do require all students to wear closed-toe shoes and long pants to class for protection against glass shards.

We understand that life happens. We offer one "make-up" studio hour per student for multi-week courses. This allows you to come in before the next scheduled class to catch up on your project. If you miss multiple classes, we cannot guarantee you will finish your piece in the allotted time.

Due to the handling of sharp glass, lead solder, and extreme heat from soldering irons, all workshop participants must be 16 years of age or older. Participants under 18 must be accompanied by a participating adult.
